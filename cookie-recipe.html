History of cookies

cookies appear to have their origins in the 7th century AD Persia, shortly after the use of sugar became relatively common in the region. they spread to Europe through the muslim conquest of spain by the 14th century, they were common in all levels of society throughout europe, from royal cuisine to street vendors

With global travel becoming widespread at that time, cookies made a natural travel companion, a modernized equivalent of the travel cakes used through out the history. one of the most popular early cookies, which traveled especially well and became known on every continent by similar names, was the jumble, a relatively hard cookie made largely from nuts, sweeterner, and water

the most common mordern cookie, given its style by creaming of butter and sugar, was not common until the 18th century

Ingredients

  • Coconut
  • Butter
  • white sugar
  • Brown sugar
  • Eggs
  • Vanilla
  • Flour
  • Baking soda
  • salt
  • Chocolate chips
  • oatmeal

Directions

  1. pre heat the oven up to 325.
  2. beat butter, sugar,eggs and vanilla till they get creamy
  3. mix together salt baking soda and flour in a separate bowl
  4. add flour mixture to butter mixture slowly
  5. bake for ten minutesor until golden Brown
